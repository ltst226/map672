A Discussion of Mapping in the New World of the Internet

The excerpt from A History of Spaces: Cartographic Reason, Mapping and the Geo-Coded World makes me think both of the history and future of cartography. In the past, the making of a map was done with what they believed at the time. Maps in the past, were not always the most accurate drawings that were completed; however, it was done to what they knew and believed. They drew their lines, showing borders to their kingdoms and points for places of importance. That is what their world was known as. These maps were created in a 2 dimensional perspective. In the present and in the future, maps have became 3 dimensional. Not only can we see a flat map that shows the world, but we can zoom in and see the height of mountains and buildings. While looking at the map on the webpage, it shows the earth as it is known, a round lump of mass, instead of a flat land mass. All of this technology in mapping can give the perspective that is given in the excerpt of a God's eye of view. With today's maps we can see everything and in every way.
